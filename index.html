🛠️ Layer 4 — BUILD YOUR OWN signal — a DIY velocity detector — the strongest, most niche-precise source
The paid tools show what everyone sees, broadly and with a lag. A detector you own watches your niche and flags what is accelerating right now — before it goes broad. That head start is the whole point.
The principle: not absolute likes — velocity (engagement per hour), filtered to your niche, ranked by acceleration. The same signal the paid tools sell, tuned to exactly your category and yours alone.
The 5-step build
1
Poller
A cron job every 15–30 min queries your niche filter (keywords + min-engagement) against a third-party X search API or the YouTube search API.
2
Snapshot store
A tiny SQLite table: post-id → [(time, likes, retweets, bookmarks)]. Each poll appends a row.
3
Velocity calc
Δ engagement ÷ Δ time. Rank by acceleration, not absolute count — a post gaining fast beats one that is already big.
4
Filter gate
“in my topic and rate above threshold” = a hit. Exactly the signal you want.
5
Real-time output
The top-accelerating posts of the last few hours = your private “what is pulling now” feed. Pipe it to a dashboard or an alert.

🎯 Playbook: competitor teardown — reverse-engineer the ad-spend the category already validated

1
Find the competitors' live ads — free
Open the Meta Ad Library (public) and pull the video ads currently running for the category leaders — e.g. Loop, Alpine, HearProtek and other earplug / hearing-protection brands. Still running = still spending = still working.
Keep it simple — you don't need a scraper to start. The Ad Library UI plus a few screen-recordings covers the first pass. Automate the pull (a script or n8n) only once you're doing this weekly — not before. A competitor-analysis workflow is likely already in place; this is the same muscle, pointed at ads instead of listings.
2
Tear the hooks apart with AI
Drop each competitor video into the Claude Code + Gemini teardown skill (~$0.27 per video). It returns the hook (first 3 seconds), the angle, the pain point, on-screen text verbatim, and a beat-by-beat breakdown.
Run it across 10–20 ads and the pattern emerges: which angles the category is actually winning with — not what you'd guess. This is the 'creative intelligence' step: understanding WHY an ad lands before you spend a cent making your own.
3
Map winning angles to your SKUs
Each angle maps to a use-case: concerts / festival hearing damage → music earplugs · can't sleep / snoring partner → sleep earplugs · open-office noise / focus → work earplugs · kids + noise sensitivity → kids earplugs.
Pick the 2–3 angles with the most ad-spend behind them (most ads, longest-running = most validated) and ignore the long tail. That's the entire payoff of the teardown: let the category's own spend tell you where to aim, instead of testing blindly.
4
Generate fresh variants on your own product
Feed a product photo plus the winning angle into a platform (MakeUGC / Linah) or your own agentic pipeline → dozens of UGC variants per angle. Test, keep the winners, kill the rest.
You're not copying their ad — you're reverse-engineering the ANGLE their spend already proved, then executing it on your product. Creative intelligence beats raw generation: 5 targeted variants on a validated angle beat 50 blind ones. Made-in-Germany / premium positioning is itself an angle worth testing here.
The lever: you're not guessing what works — you reverse-engineer ad-spend the category has already validated. Creative intelligence (knowing WHY an ad lands) beats raw generation (making more ads blindly). Open the green button on each step for the simple how-to.

🔬 The competitor-teardown skill, in depth — the sharpest single tool for a small team
The idea in one line: instead of a media buyer scrubbing competitor ads frame by frame — pausing, screenshotting on-screen text, and by the tenth video forgetting what made the first one land — you drop the video into Claude Code and get a structured creative teardown back in minutes. It's the closest thing here to a shortcut, because it turns "study what's already working" from half a day of manual work into a repeatable command.
HowDrop any video file — a competitor UGC ad, a Meta ad, an organic TikTok, a produced ad, even a Loom / screen-recording — into Claude Code. The skill routes it through the Gemini API for native video understanding: Gemini actually watches the footage, it doesn't just read a transcript.
OutputA structured teardown, not a summary: hook breakdown (the first 3 seconds) · angle + target audience · pain point + CTA · beat-by-beat scene structure · verbatim on-screen text and dialogue with timestamps · and hook variations generated directly from the competitor's own ad — steal-worthy patterns to apply to your own creative.
EdgeNative video understanding beats a transcript tool: it reads the on-screen text, the pacing and the visual hook, not only the spoken words. That's the difference between a rough summary and an actual creative breakdown — and why the output is usable, not just informational.
KillsReverse-engineering competitor ads is a weekly job for media buyers, and by hand it's slow and lossy. This makes it fast, structured and repeatable — the same skill works across UGC ads, produced ads, TikToks and Looms.
